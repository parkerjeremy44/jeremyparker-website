Fluence Energy


My main role was to modernize and improve the user experience of the Fluence Energy internal application. After presenting the UX research and wireframes, I was promoted with the secondary role of Product Owner. From this point, I hired a React engineer and a Junior UX designer to complete the team. I also managed an intern and delegated them the work of documentation in Jira/Confluence as well as tracking our team logs.

My journey started with a visit to an onsite location of the Fluence main product, the cube battery rack system.


I spoke with Commissioning Engineers, Mechanical Engineers, Electrical Engineers, and documented their every day work experience and job duties. This gave me insight into how they worked the cube system, which in turn allowed me to design for them directly so that the application would be intuitive to their nature and knowledge.

Design for Customer Experience while keeping a technical base of operation for Commissioning Engineers in the application.

From there I spent the next few weeks collecting our thoughts from the site visit and creating a wireframe with all of the components necessary for the engineers to run the system effectively. Table View


Create a new table view design with loads of new functionality intent on being powerful, fast, and smartly presented.

Using the Audit screen as shown above as our start point, this is one of the early iterations. I was testing out different ways of showing breadcrumbs and how to display a way to change views in the workable area. Some early design decisions such as removing the separating line between the first few columns were to divide the columns of data from each element or piece of the Cube.

You can also see some ideas where the user could drill down into the Core as a whole while also using the Table View. This would allow overhead control and visible data when moving through different parts of the system.
